BDS (Bachelor of Dental Surgery) is India's second-most-sought medical course after MBBS, offering students a faster pathway to financial independence through private dental practice. With approximately 27,000 seats across government and private dental colleges nationwide, BDS provides strong accessibility for NEET-qualified students.

Top states for BDS management quota admission include Maharashtra, Karnataka, Rajasthan, and Gujarat, each hosting well-established dental colleges with modern clinical training facilities.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the duration of the BDS course? +
BDS (Bachelor of Dental Surgery) is a 5-year program: 4 years of academic study followed by a mandatory 1-year rotary internship in clinical dental practice.
What NEET score is required for BDS? +
BDS requires the basic NEET qualifying score — 117 for General category and 93 for SC/ST/OBC (2024 cutoffs) — the same minimum requirement as MBBS, though specific colleges may set their own minimums for management quota seats.
What are the career options after BDS? +
BDS graduates can register with the Dental Council of India and begin private practice immediately, pursue MDS (Master of Dental Surgery) for specialisation, open their own dental clinic, or join corporate dental chains.
Is BDS a good career choice in 2025? +
Yes. Dental healthcare demand is rising steadily in India, and BDS offers a faster route to financial independence through private practice compared to the longer MBBS-to-specialisation pathway.
What is the fee range for BDS in India? +
Private dental college management quota fees typically range from ₹3 lakhs to ₹8 lakhs per year, varying by state and college reputation.
