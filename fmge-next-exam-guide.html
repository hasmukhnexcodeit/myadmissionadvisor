FMGE / NEXT Exam Guide for MBBS Abroad Graduates

Everything you need to know about the licensing exam required to practice medicine in India after completing MBBS abroad.

📋 Get Free Counselling

What Is FMGE?

The Foreign Medical Graduate Examination (FMGE) is a screening test administered by the National Board of Examinations that every Indian citizen who completed their MBBS (or equivalent) degree from a foreign medical institution must clear in order to register for medical practice in India. It is a single-paper, objective-type exam covering the entire MBBS curriculum.

The Transition to NEXT

India's National Medical Commission has introduced the National Exit Test (NEXT) as a unified licensing exam that will eventually serve as both the final qualifying exam for MBBS graduates from Indian colleges and the licensing exam for those who studied abroad — replacing FMGE entirely once fully implemented.

Why University Choice Matters for FMGE Success

FMGE pass rates vary dramatically by university — not just by country. While the national average sits around 45%, students from top-tier universities like Kazan Federal University (Russia) or Tbilisi State Medical University (Georgia) have historically shown pass rates of 60-72% among MyAdmissionAdvisor-placed students. This is precisely why we emphasise university selection, not just country selection, during counselling.

How MyAdmissionAdvisor Supports FMGE Preparation

We maintain tie-ups with leading FMGE coaching institutes and provide ongoing guidance to students throughout their MBBS abroad — not just at the admission stage. This includes connecting final-year students with structured coaching programs timed to their graduation, and sharing subject-wise preparation strategies based on patterns from previous exam cycles.

Exam Structure

FMGE consists of 300 multiple-choice questions covering all subjects from the MBBS curriculum, conducted as a computer-based test. A minimum of 50% (150 marks) is required to pass. The exam is held twice yearly, typically in June and December.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is FMGE and who needs to take it? +
FMGE (Foreign Medical Graduate Examination) is a licensing exam that all Indian students who completed their MBBS/MD abroad must clear to obtain registration to practice medicine in India. It is being transitioned to a new exam called NEXT.
What is the difference between FMGE and NEXT? +
NEXT (National Exit Test) is a unified licensing exam being phased in to eventually replace both FMGE (for foreign graduates) and the final-year MBBS exam (for Indian graduates), creating a single standardised qualifying exam for all MBBS graduates regardless of where they studied.
How many attempts are allowed for FMGE/NEXT? +
The exam is conducted twice a year (typically June and December), and there is currently no cap on the number of attempts, allowing candidates to retake the exam until they pass.
Which countries have the best FMGE pass rates? +
Pass rates vary significantly by university rather than just by country. Top-tier universities in Georgia and select Russian institutions like Kazan Federal University have historically shown stronger pass rates (60-72%) compared to the national average (around 45%).
