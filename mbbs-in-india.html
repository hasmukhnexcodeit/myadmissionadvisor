Frequently Asked Questions

What is management quota in MBBS admission? +
Private medical colleges are permitted by the National Medical Commission to fill a certain percentage of seats (typically 15–50%) through their own management process, separate from government NEET counselling. These management quota seats are filled by applying directly to the college, often through a consultant like MyAdmissionAdvisor who has established relationships with admission offices.
Do I need a high NEET score for management quota seats? +
No. Management quota only requires the basic NEET qualifying marks — there is no higher merit-based cutoff like government counselling. However, individual colleges may set their own minimum score thresholds (typically 150–250) for management quota seats, which we help you navigate.
How quickly can a management quota seat be confirmed? +
MyAdmissionAdvisor can typically confirm a management quota MBBS seat in 2–5 business days from the time you provide all required documents and arrange the seat confirmation fee payment.
Is a management quota MBBS degree recognised the same way as a government quota degree? +
Yes, absolutely. All MBBS degrees from NMC-recognised colleges in India carry equal recognition — there is no distinction in the degree certificate or its validity between government quota and management quota admissions.
When do management quota seats typically open? +
Most management quota seats open after the second or final round of NEET state counselling concludes, typically in September or October. Exact timing varies by state and college.
What is the difference between management quota and NRI quota? +
Management quota seats are available to any Indian citizen meeting NEET qualifying marks. NRI quota seats are specifically reserved for NRI, PIO, or OCI candidates and typically carry higher fees but have a separate, often less competitive, application pool.
