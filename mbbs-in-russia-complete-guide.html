MBBS in Russia 2025: Complete Guide for Indian Students

June 15, 2025

Russia remains the most popular MBBS abroad destination for Indian students, and for good reason — it offers the lowest total cost among NMC-recognised options globally, combined with a long, established track record of Indian students successfully completing their medical education there.

Why Russia Continues to Lead

With over 18,000 Indian students currently enrolled across Russian medical universities, the infrastructure for Indian students — from food to cultural support — is more developed in Russia than almost anywhere else. Tuition fees starting at $3,500/year keep total six-year costs as low as ₹18 lakhs, dramatically less than private medical colleges in India.

Choosing the Right University

Not all NMC-approved universities are equal. FMGE pass rates vary significantly — from around 45% nationally to over 70% for graduates of top-tier universities like Kazan Federal University. This is precisely why working with an experienced consultancy matters: the difference between a 45% and 72% FMGE pass rate can determine whether years of study translate into a practicing medical career in India.

What to Expect

The first year typically focuses on foundational sciences alongside Russian language classes. Years two through four cover core medical subjects, while years five and six involve substantial clinical rotations in university-affiliated hospitals. Throughout, English remains the medium of instruction for all academic coursework.
