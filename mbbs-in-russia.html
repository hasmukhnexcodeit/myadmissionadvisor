Russia has been a top destination for Indian medical aspirants since the 1960s, and today over 18,000 Indian students are enrolled in Russian medical universities recognised by India's National Medical Commission (NMC). With tuition fees starting at just $3,500 per year and a total six-year cost as low as ₹18 lakhs, MBBS in Russia offers the lowest overall cost among all European MBBS destinations — without compromising on education quality.

Unlike private medical colleges in India that charge ₹50 lakhs to ₹1 crore through management quota, Russian universities have a single transparent fee structure published by the government. There is no donation, no capitation fee, and no management quota — every student, Indian or otherwise, pays the same published tuition.

Frequently Asked Questions — MBBS in Russia

Is MBBS from Russia valid in India? +
Yes. Degrees from NMC-approved Russian universities are fully valid in India. After completing MBBS, graduates must clear the FMGE/NEXT exam to obtain a licence to practice. We only recommend universities that appear on the official NMC-approved list.
Is there any donation or management quota required for Russia MBBS? +
No. Russian medical universities have a single, government-published fee structure with no donation, capitation fee, or management quota. Every applicant — Indian or international — pays the same tuition fee for their chosen university.
What is the medium of instruction in Russian medical universities? +
All lectures, textbooks, and examinations are conducted in English for the MBBS programme. Russian language classes are compulsory for 2–3 years to help students communicate with patients during clinical rotations, but Russian is not tested in the FMGE exam.
How safe is Russia for Indian students? +
Russian university cities such as Kazan, Kursk, and Orenburg have large, well-established Indian student communities. University campuses have dedicated security and Indian student associations actively support newcomers with orientation and emergency assistance.
Is Indian food available in Russia? +
Yes. Most major university cities have Indian restaurants and grocery stores stocking Indian spices, rice, dal, and other staples. University hostels typically include shared kitchen facilities, and many students cook their own meals.
What is the total cost of MBBS in Russia including living expenses? +
For budget-friendly universities like Kazan or Orenburg, the total 6-year cost (tuition + hostel + food + miscellaneous) is approximately ₹18–24 lakhs. For premium Moscow-based universities, it can range from ₹35–45 lakhs. We provide a detailed year-by-year cost breakdown during your free counselling session.
How cold does it get in Russia, and is that a problem for Indian students? +
Russian winters can drop to −20°C to −30°C in cities like Kazan and Kursk. With proper winter clothing (which we guide you on purchasing), Indian students adapt within the first semester. Summers, by contrast, are pleasant and many cities are scenic.
Can I get a part-time job while studying MBBS in Russia? +
Student visas typically permit up to 20 hours of part-time work per week. However, the MBBS curriculum in Russia is intensive, and we strongly recommend prioritising studies and FMGE preparation over part-time work, especially in the clinical years.
