Nepal offers Indian students the unique advantage of pursuing an MBBS abroad without ever truly leaving the subcontinent — sharing an open border, deep cultural ties, and language similarity (Hindi is widely understood throughout Nepal). The curriculum closely mirrors India's own MBBS structure, and many of Nepal's top medical colleges are affiliated with established Indian institutions like Manipal.

While Nepal's NEET cutoff requirement (200+) is higher than Russia or Central Asian countries, and total costs are correspondingly higher (₹35–55 lakhs), many families prefer Nepal specifically for the ease of travel — a 30-minute flight or overnight bus journey from most Indian border cities means students can visit home far more frequently than peers studying in Europe or Central Asia.

Frequently Asked Questions — MBBS in Nepal

What NEET score is needed for Nepal? +
Nepal has a higher entry requirement than Russia or Kyrgyzstan — typically NEET 200+ is needed for most universities, though some institutions may accept marginally lower scores.
Can I visit India frequently while studying in Nepal? +
Yes — this is one of Nepal's biggest advantages. With just a 30-minute flight or an overnight bus from most Indian border cities, students can travel home for festivals and family events with far greater ease than from any other MBBS abroad destination.
Is the medium of instruction English? +
Yes, all Nepal medical universities teach in English. Clinical exposure is particularly smooth as patients communicate in Nepali, a language with significant similarity to Hindi.
Are Nepal MBBS degrees valid in India? +
Yes, provided the university is NMC-approved. Many practicing doctors in India today completed their MBBS in Nepal, and the FMGE/NEXT requirement applies the same as for any foreign degree.
