Postgraduate medical education in India is intensely competitive, with NEET-PG scores determining government counselling allocation. Frequently Asked Questions

What is the duration of MD/MS programs? +
MD (Doctor of Medicine) and MS (Master of Surgery) are both 3-year postgraduate programs pursued after completing MBBS and a mandatory internship.
Can I get a management quota seat for MD/MS even after NEET-PG counselling? +
Yes. MyAdmissionAdvisor specifically helps candidates who qualified NEET-PG but didn't secure their preferred speciality or location find management quota seats across our network of partner colleges.
What are the most in-demand MD/MS specialisations? +
MD Dermatology, MD Radiology, MS Orthopaedics, and MD Obstetrics & Gynaecology are among the most competitive specialisations due to strong private practice income potential and lifestyle factors.
What is the fee range for management quota MD/MS seats? +
Fees vary significantly by specialisation and college, typically ranging from ₹12 lakhs to ₹25 lakhs per year for clinical specialities in private medical colleges.
